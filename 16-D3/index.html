Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Age, Income

Smoking% and Obesity% are both found to be negatively correlated to median household income. States with the lowest median household income (MS:$39k, WV: $41k, AR: $41k) are also among the higest rates of smoking (MS: 23.0%, WV: 26.7%, AR:24.7%) and obesity (MS: 35.5%, WV: 35.7%, AR: 35.9%).

Age does not seem to be a determining factor in obesity, smoking, or lacking health insurance.

Poverty may be somewhat positively correlated to obesity, smoking, and lacking health insurance. Further research is needed to determine the extent of this relationship or whether there are additional factors contributing to the connection.
